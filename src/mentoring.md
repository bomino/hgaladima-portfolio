<section class="mentoring-section reveal">
<p class="mentoring-kicker">What I'm looking for</p>
<h2 class="mentoring-section-title">Students who bring a question, not a method.</h2>

The strongest projects I've advised started with a problem in public health the student cared about — late-stage colorectal-cancer diagnosis in underserved populations, acculturative stress among farmworkers, barriers to comprehensive HIV care — and then asked *what methods will let us say something defensible about this?* Methods follow questions, not the other way around. That's the work I'd like to do with you.

Practically: my advising sits at the intersection of **biostatistics methodology** and **applied public-health research**. Projects I'm best suited to mentor:

- **Cancer-disparities research** with an emphasis on structural and neighborhood factors — colorectal, breast, and increasingly other sites where disparities in stage-at-diagnosis and treatment pattern matter
- **Machine learning in health-outcomes research** — not ML for its own sake, but as a tool when classical regression struggles with high-dimensional predictors, complex interactions, or messy registry-based data
- **Propensity-score methods and causal inference** from observational data, particularly where diagnostic-accuracy or risk-prediction is the target
- **Complex-survey analysis** for population-level public-health research (PRAMS, BRFSS, NHIS, NSDUH)

If your question is closer to the **methodology** end — a simulation study, a new estimator, methods for small samples — I'm interested. If your question is closer to the **application** end — using existing methods well on a specific public-health problem — I'm also interested. The combination matters less than the clarity of the question.
</section>

<section class="mentoring-section mentoring-reach-out reveal">
<p class="mentoring-kicker">How to reach out</p>
<h2 class="mentoring-section-title">Email me — but write it well.</h2>

Before emailing, please check that the [Health Services Research PhD program at ODU](https://www.odu.edu/academics/academic-programs/phd-health-services-research) is a fit for your goals; this is the program I advise in. If the program fit is right, an initial email from you should include:

<ol class="mentoring-checklist">
<li><strong>What public-health problem interests you</strong> — 2–3 sentences in your own words. Not a method, not a dataset — a problem you want to understand.</li>
<li><strong>Why you're writing to me specifically</strong> — one or two sentences. If you've read a paper of mine that resonated, name it. If you haven't yet, that's fine — tell me what about my research program matches what you want to do.</li>
<li><strong>Your current stage and timeline</strong> — are you applying for Fall 2026 / 2027 / etc.? Are you already at ODU on a different funding line? Are you exploring multiple PIs?</li>
<li><strong>Your CV or a short background paragraph</strong> — prior training, relevant skills (statistical programming in R / SAS / Python; any biostats or epidemiology coursework), and anything else that would give me a sense of where you're starting.</li>
</ol>

Send the email to <a href="mailto:consulting@hgaladima.com">consulting@hgaladima.com</a>. Please put <em>"PhD advising inquiry"</em> in the subject line so I can prioritize response — I try to reply to these within a week during the academic year, longer in summer.
</section>
